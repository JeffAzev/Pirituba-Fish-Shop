Tuna Crusted

    Ingredients:

    Tuna

  • 170g of Tuna

  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup black sesame seeds

  • 1/4 cup white sesame seeds


    • Mushroom Rice

  • 500g mushroom
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 cups water

    Tuna Instructions:

  1. Mix the white and black sesame seeds on a shallow plate.
  2. Season the tuna steaks with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Dip the tuna steaks in olive oil, ensuring they are fully coated.
  4. Press each side of the tuna steaks into the sesame seeds, covering them evenly.
  5. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and add a tablespoon of olive oil.
  6. Sear the tuna steaks for 45 seconds to 1 minute on each side, or until the sesame seeds are lightly toasted and the tuna is cooked to your desired doneness (the interior should remain pink).
  7. Remove the tuna steaks from the skillet and set aside.

    Mushroom Rice Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add 1/2 onion and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add all mushrooms and cook until golden brown.
  4. Remove some mushrooms from the skillet and set aside for garnish.
  5. Add the rice to the skillet and sauté.
  6. Pour the water into the skillet and season with salt to taste.
  7. Cook the rice over medium-low heat until all the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is cooked.
  8. Once the rice is ready, add the reserved mushrooms on top for garnish.
  9. Serve the grilled tuna with the hot mushroom rice.

Stuffed Seabass

    Ingredients:

  • 1 whole Seabass

  • olives

  • parsley

  • 5 cloves of garlic

  • 1 lemon

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

  • Broccolis

  • Asparguss

    Instructions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the chopped parsley, minced garlic, and lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Make three to four diagonal cuts on the skin side of the sea bass, being careful not to cut too deep.
  4. Spread the parsley, garlic, and lemon mixture inside the cavities of the fish.
  5. Place some olives inside the fish, along with the parsley and garlic mixture.
  6. Drizzle the fish with one tablespoon of extra virgin olive oil and spread the remaining over the fish
  7. Place the sea bass on a baking tray.
  8. Arrange the vegetables around the fish on the baking tray.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  10. Remove from the oven and serve the stuffed sea bass hot, accompanied by the roasted vegetables.

Salmon Wellington

    Ingredients:
  • 2 Salmons Darnes

  • 2 Sheets of Puffy Pastry

  • 300g Mushrooms

  • 500g Spinach

  • 1 tablespoon Dijon Mustard

  • Parmesan Cheese

  • 1 Egg

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

  • 2 cloves of garlic

  • Sesame Seeds

    Instructions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add chopped mushrooms to the skillet and cook until golden brown. Then, add grated Parmesan cheese and stir until incorporated. Set aside.
  4. Roll out the puff pastry sheets and brush a thin layer of Dijon mustard on each.
  5. Place a salmon fillet on top of the mustard on each puff pastry sheet.
  6. Spoon the mushroom and Parmesan mixture over the salmon fillets.
  7. Sprinkle a little more grated Parmesan cheese on top of the mushroom mixture.
  8. Fold the puff pastry over the salmon and press the edges to seal.
  9. Brush the top of the puff pastry with egg wash.
  10. If desired, sprinkle some sesame seeds on top of the puff pastry.
  11. Place the Salmon Wellingtons on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  12. Remove from the oven, let them rest for a few minutes, and serve.
